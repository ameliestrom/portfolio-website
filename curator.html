The Curator

  • Role: UI Designer
  • Platforms: Android and iOS
  • Duration: Four Weeks
  • Tools: Sketch, InVision, Keynote, and Pen & Paper

Problem Statement: Upcoming artists, and creatives are normally struggling in the beginning of their career with the question, how? How do I market my own work? How do I find ways for new people to view my work? How do I invite people to my exhibitions? As there’s an astounding amount of upcoming artists available from various cultures, excited to share their own artwork, there’s a space needed to find them to be able to explore your own creativity, motivation, or simply, your own way of relaxing, and rewinding.

Solution: The Curator is an app concept aimed for anyone who wants to share their own creativity. Newcoming artists are able to market their own artwork, and upcoming exhibitions by using The Curator. The app is aimed for art enthusiasts as well who want to explore new, and upcoming art exhibitions in their homecity, or while they are traveling. Users are also able to purchase art, and support artists who are sharing their talent on The Curator.

My Design Process

As The Curator was a project concept I came up with from my personal interest, I defined each stage I had to go through to be able to develop my understanding, and knowledge further.

1. Discovery

Competitive Analysis

To understand the potential users and their problems while using location based apps, I focused on competitive research. I did this by identifying the Strengths, Weaknesses, Pain Points and Key Features of Artland, and Artsy.

2. Define

Key Features

  • User is able to view upcoming art exhibitions near them, or in a specific city selected.
  • User is able to add an art exhibition to their calendar
  • Artist is able to upload an upcoming event.

3. Ideation

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

After having completed the user research, and definition of the app, I was able to begin exploring through sketching my low-fidelity wireframes.

Wireframes

After brainstorming, and sketching ideas according to the user flow diagrams planned, I began moving the sketches to mid-fidelity wireframes. These wireframes were designed on Sketch. They aimed to present the layout, features, and user flows while using the app.

iOS and Android Wireframes

4. UI

Moodboard

To begin establishing an aesthetic that would be suitable according to The Curator’s aspirations, and mindset, I began exploring the interior, and design of museums, and art studios. I began researching, and comparing various artists studios, and their websites to understand the general aesthetic that the users are familiar with.

Color Palette

5. Prototype & Testing

Recap

As I am personally very interested in exploring new art exhibitions, this project was very exciting to design. I was able to put myself into the shoes of the user in a different way as this is a app I would want to use myself. The competitive analysis was very interesting to research as there weren’t a lot of options in terms of art exhibition locater apps. This made the analysis even more important as there weren’t a wide variety of comparisons available to make. The process was very smooth which I believe was due to my planning from early on in the project. I set up my design process from the beginning, and knew the steps I wanted to take, and what I wanted to complete for tasks. Furthermore, I learnt about designing for Android on another level, which I really appreciate. I was able to take time to study the Material Design Guidelines, and interview Android users who helped me understand the difference in more detail.

The Curator Screens

Sign In/Sign Up

Onboarding

The onboarding process allows the user to personalize their account in more depth while setting up their account. The onboarding process also allows the user to find suggested artists to follow to allow them a head start with their account.

Navigation

There’s a simple navigation system which allows artist users to upload a post, search the app, and access their profile.

Edit Profile

The user will be able to edit their account details simply by clicking the dots menu in the upper right corner. The settings bar will appear with the options available to edit.

Detailed

Each screen is focused on minimalism and being simple to navigate and use.
